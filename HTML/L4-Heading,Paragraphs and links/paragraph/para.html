Tiger Documentary

This is a documentary about the majestic and endangered tigers, the largest cats in the world.

Tigers are native to Asia and live in various habitats, such as forests, grasslands, and swamps. They are apex predators and hunt mostly deer, wild boar, and buffalo. They can weigh up to 300 kg and measure up to 3.3 m in length.

Tigers are also highly social and territorial animals. They communicate with each other using vocalizations, scent marks, and body language. They form bonds with their mates and offspring, and sometimes share their kills with other tigers.

Unfortunately, tigers are facing many threats from humans, such as poaching, habitat loss, and conflict. There are only about 3,900 tigers left in the wild, and they are classified as endangered by the IUCN. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these magnificent creatures and their habitats.
